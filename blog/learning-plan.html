Formulate a learning plan

In Sprint 2, we were asked to formulate a learning plan that addresses the following elements:

  • Long-term career goal/pathway
  • Learning strengths and limitations
  • Non-technical/core skills to develop further
  • Commitment to how I will manage my workload
  • How and where will I seek help in a timely way
  • My expectations from the Facilitation Team
  • Identify any scheduling issues

I discuss each of these in more detail below.

Long-term career goal/pathway

I am currently working as an accountant, but my role is no longer full-time. Rather than finding additional accounting work, I aim to use DevAcademy as a way to transition from this to a new career as a Web Developer.

My plan, at the conclusion of this course, is to return to full-time employment, by seeking part-time web-development work (up to 50%).

Over time, as my web development skills, experience and earning potential all increase (and on the assumption that I am good at it and enjoy it!), I will gradually look to move more away from accounting, towards full-time web development.

But who knows, there may also be opportunities to combine the skillsets (hello Xero! ;-)

I expect that this will take some time (e.g. possibly 3-5 years).

Learning strengths and limitations

During foundations, I have discovered that I learn better by observing (e.g. video examples) and doing (practicing), as opposed to reading long articles or technical guides.

Obviously there will be times when I need to do both, but I think overall, the style of learning at DevAcademy should suit me well.

Non-technical/core skills to develop further

It would be good to work on becoming more assertive and direct with people.

Commitment to how I will manage my workload

This question is in two parts:

How will I manage myself to work productively and safely with others?

I will attempt to honour any commitments I make to team mates and will communicate my progress clearly.

What will I do when things are building up and starting to get to me?

If I am struggling, I will make sure people who may be relying on me know and will seek help accordingly. I might also take some time out to de-stress, by doing something I enjoy (exercise, music, spending time with family/friends), to clear my head.

How and where will I seek help in a timely way?

Depending on the nature of the issue, I will raise it with the facilitation team, team mates, or family and friends, as appropriate.

What do I expect from the facilitation team?

I don't expect all members of the faciliation team to be available at all times, but I do expect that they make it clear who is available to help and when.

Identify any scheduling issues, when I am committed to other things

I have made it clear to family and friends that, during bootcamp, I will need to be fully committed to my studies. However, there will be times when things crop up that I will have to give priority to.

I will give as much advance notice of these times as possible.
